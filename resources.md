# Additional Resources

A short list of resources for going deeper on wetware computing. (Not exhaustive — PRs welcome.) This is one of the youngest and smallest fields in this repo collection — treat any specific capability claim as a snapshot in time, and note that public/media coverage of this field is particularly prone to overstating current capability, so prioritize primary, peer-reviewed sources over popular coverage.

## Foundational Reading
- Kagan et al., "In vitro neurons learn and exhibit sentience when embodied in a simulated game-world" (Neuron, 2022) — the widely-discussed "DishBrain" paper reporting closed-loop stimulation results in cultured neurons; read the actual methods and results sections, not just summaries, given how widely (and sometimes loosely) this paper's framing has been discussed in secondary coverage.
- Smirnova et al., "Organoid intelligence (OI): the new frontier in biocomputing and intelligence-in-a-dish" (Frontiers in Science, 2023) — a foundational paper specifically framing and motivating the organoid intelligence research direction.
- Friston, "The free-energy principle: a unified brain theory?" (Nature Reviews Neuroscience, 2010) — foundational reading for the theoretical framework discussed in section 4, useful for understanding both its content and its status as one actively-debated theoretical account among others in computational neuroscience.
- Companion **Computational Biologist** repo — relevant for general statistical rigor, small-sample-size considerations, and computational modeling practices this repo builds on.

## Bioethics Reading (section 7)
- Published guidance and position statements from major bioethics bodies and academic institutions specifically addressing brain organoid research ethics — search for current, institution-specific guidance rather than relying on any single source, given how actively this area is developing.
- Look specifically for pieces representing a range of perspectives on the precautionary principle's application to this field, consistent with this repo's approach of presenting genuine debate rather than a single position.

## Key Technologies (good to be conversationally familiar with)
- Microelectrode array (MEA) systems commonly used for in vitro neural recording and stimulation.
- Spike-sorting software and algorithms commonly used in electrophysiology research.
- Brain organoid differentiation protocols and the broader stem-cell-derived neural tissue literature.

## Communities & Discussion
- Major computational neuroscience and bioengineering conferences increasingly feature sessions specifically on organoid intelligence and wetware computing.
- r/neuroscience and r/singularity for broader (though variably rigorous) discussion threads — cross-check claims encountered here against primary literature.

## Companion Repos (adjacent, complementary content)
- **Computational Biologist** — statistical rigor, small-sample considerations, and computational modeling practices relevant throughout this repo.
- **Engineered Living Materials** — relevant background on sustaining living biological systems outside their native context, discussed there for material applications but conceptually parallel to sections 2 and 6 here.
- **Foundation-Model Scientist (BioAI)** — relevant for the general discipline of validating computational/ML claims about biological systems rigorously, applicable to section 5.

## Staying Current
- This field is genuinely very young and moves quickly relative to its small size — a small number of active research groups and companies account for most published work, and following their recent publications directly is more reliable than relying on secondary or media coverage.
- Ethical oversight frameworks specific to this field are actively being developed by research institutions and professional bodies — check current, institution-specific guidance rather than assuming a static, settled framework exists.
