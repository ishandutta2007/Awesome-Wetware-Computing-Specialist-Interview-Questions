# Wetware Computing Specialist Interview Questions 🧠💻

A curated, community-driven collection of interview questions (with model answers, frameworks, and explanations) for roles working on **wetware computing** — using cultured living neurons or brain organoids as a computing substrate, sometimes called "organoid intelligence" or "biological computing."

> 📝 **A note on the title:** "Wetware Computing Specialist" isn't a standardized industry job title. This repo covers the real, active (if very young and small) research field this title points to — titles you're more likely to actually see include "Research Scientist, Organoid Intelligence," "Neural Interface Engineer," or "Bioengineer" at one of a small number of specialized labs and startups working in this space.

This is not a list of trivia. Every question includes:
- **Why interviewers ask it**
- **A model answer or framework**
- **Follow-up questions** interviewers commonly use to probe deeper

> 🌱 This is v1. Contributions, corrections, and new questions are very welcome — see [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

> ⚠️ **Note on scope:** This is one of the youngest and smallest fields in this repo collection — demonstrated computational capability to date is very limited (e.g., simple game-playing or pattern-discrimination tasks), and this repo is deliberately honest about that throughout rather than implying near-term practical computing applications. This field also raises genuinely significant, actively-debated bioethics questions about the moral status of cultured neural tissue and organoids. Consistent with this repo's contribution guidelines, ethics content presents the real range of expert perspectives rather than asserting a single settled position — these are open questions in bioethics and philosophy of mind, not solved problems. This repo builds on neuroscience, electrophysiology, and machine learning fundamentals; some familiarity with the companion **Computational Biologist** and **Foundation-Model Scientist (BioAI)** repos may help for the modeling-adjacent sections.

## 🧭 How to Use This Repo

- **Coming from a neuroscience/electrophysiology background?** Prioritize sections 5 and 6 — the goal is building fluency in the computational/ML and systems-engineering side of interfacing with living neural tissue.
- **Coming from a computer engineering/ML background?** Prioritize sections 2 and 3 — you'll need working fluency in neural culture biology and electrophysiological interfacing before your computing background is usefully applied to this domain.
- **Interviewing at a lab or company working on organoid-based computing specifically?** Focus heavily on sections 2 and 4.
- **Interviewing for a role touching public communication, funding, or ethics review?** Focus heavily on section 7.

Each question is tagged with a rough difficulty and role-level indicator:
- 🟢 Junior/PhD-entry · 🟡 Mid-level Researcher · 🔴 Senior/Principal Researcher
